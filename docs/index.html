Support

Sudokubot lets you photograph or select a picture of a printed Sudoku puzzle, automatically recognizes the digits, and solves it — all on your device.

How to use it

  • Scan a puzzle: tap the camera icon on the home screen and photograph a printed Sudoku grid, or choose an existing photo from your library.
  • Check the recognized digits: after scanning, review the detected board and use the numpad to correct any misread cells before solving.
  • Solve: tap Solve to see the completed board and, if needed, step-by-step hints.
  • Archive: save a solved puzzle to your on-device archive to revisit it later; swipe left on an archive entry to delete it.

Troubleshooting

The app misreads some digits. Recognition works best with a well-lit, flat, non-glare photo taken straight-on. Use the numpad on the review screen to fix any incorrect cells before solving.

The camera or photo picker won't open. Check that Sudokubot has Camera and Photo Library permission under Settings → Sudokubot on your device.

Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 9, 2026

Sudokubot ("the app") is designed to work entirely on your device. This policy explains what data the app touches and, just as importantly, what it does not do with it.

Summary

Sudokubot does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data. It has no servers, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party SDKs. Everything the app does happens locally on your device.

Camera and Photo Library

Sudokubot requests access to your Camera and Photo Library solely to let you capture or select an image of a printed Sudoku puzzle. That image is processed on-device to recognize the puzzle's digits and is never uploaded, saved to your photo library, or shared with anyone — it exists only transiently in memory while the app is solving your puzzle.

Data stored on your device

If you choose to save a solved puzzle to your archive, the app stores only the puzzle's solution, hints, your notes, and a timestamp in a local file on your device. This data:

  • never leaves your device;
  • is not linked to any account, since Sudokubot has no accounts or sign-in;
  • can be deleted at any time by swiping to delete an archive entry, or removed entirely by deleting the app.

Third parties

Sudokubot does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, or crash-reporting services, and makes no network requests of any kind.

Children's privacy

Sudokubot does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13, because it does not collect personal information from any user.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.
